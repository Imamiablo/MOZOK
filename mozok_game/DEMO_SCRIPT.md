# 5-minute demo script

## Pitch

"This is not the final game. This is the aquarium for MOZOK: a first-person grid island where agents have bodies, needs, memory-facing events, emotions, and turn-based behaviour."

## Scene

The player wakes up in a tiny island camp. Alice wants to understand the cave. Boris protects supplies. Mira wants everyone near the fire.

## Steps

1. Turn and step through the grid from the Class-of-Heroes-style pseudo-3D view.
2. Interact with the food crate.
3. Explain that taking food creates a world event.
4. Wait a few turns.
5. Show agents moving towards water, food, shelter, fire, or cave depending on their needs.
6. Stand next to Alice or Mira, press `T`, and type a free-text question.
7. Explain that every adjacent agent gets its own `/chat` call and responds from its own context.
8. Point at the side party panels and bottom brain panel: emotion, cognitive focus, and rationale are visible as agent state.
9. Choose a quick dialogue topic with `E` and show that conversation can surface memory, intent, trust, fear, or suspicion.
10. Inspect the cave, radio, or food crate and point at memory flashes.
11. Press `Tab` to show the debug overlay.
12. Explain: "These are the signals we will feed to MOZOK. The game owns physics; MOZOK owns meaning and intention."

## Best demo moment

Take food from the crate, wait, and explain that Boris is the kind of agent who should later remember and resent this if supplies run low.
